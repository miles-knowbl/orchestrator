# Budget Signals Reference

Types of budget indicators and how to interpret them.

## Explicit Budget Signals

### Exact Amount

**What it looks like:**
- "$200,000"
- "Half a million dollars"
- "1.2M"
- "Two hundred thousand"

**Confidence:** High — they stated a number

**Interpretation:** This is their budget. May be ceiling, target, or starting point depending on context.

### Range

**What it looks like:**
- "$200K-$400K"
- "Between 100 and 200 thousand"
- "In the 500K range"
- "Around a quarter million, maybe up to 300"

**Confidence:** High — they stated a range

**Interpretation:** Lower end is often the real budget, upper end is stretch.

---

## Indicator Budget Signals

### Magnitude Indicator

**What it looks like:**
- "Six figures"
- "Seven figures"
- "Significant investment"
- "Substantial budget"
- "Major initiative"

**Confidence:** Medium — indicates order of magnitude

**Interpretation:**
- "Six figures" = $100K-$999K (wide range)
- "Seven figures" = $1M+
- "Significant" = varies by company size

### Company Size Proxy

**What it looks like:**
- Company size: 500 employees, $50M revenue
- Industry benchmarks available
- Similar companies mentioned

**Confidence:** Low — inference from proxy data

**Interpretation:** Use industry benchmarks. Enterprise = larger budget than SMB.

---

## Approval Status Signals

### Budget Approved

**What it looks like:**
- "Budget is approved"
- "Finance signed off"
- "Already allocated in our plan"
- "We have the money"
- "CFO approved"

**Confidence:** High — explicit approval

**Interpretation:** Money is available. Focus on timeline and fit.

### Needs Approval

**What it looks like:**
- "Need to get approval"
- "Have to run it by finance"
- "Need CFO sign-off"
- "Will need a business case"
- "Haven't secured budget yet"

**Confidence:** Medium — process known, outcome unknown

**Interpretation:** Budget is a gate. Understand approval process and help build the case.

### Uncertain

**What it looks like:**
- "Not sure about budget yet"
- "Still figuring out the numbers"
- "Depends on what we decide"
- No budget discussion at all

**Confidence:** Low — unknown

**Interpretation:** Need more discovery. May not be ready to buy.

---

## Constraint Signals

### Limited Budget

**What it looks like:**
- "Budget is tight"
- "Limited funds this year"
- "Need to be cost-conscious"
- "Not a lot of room"

**Confidence:** Medium — constraint identified

**Interpretation:** Will need to prove ROI, offer creative pricing, or wait.

### Competing Priorities

**What it looks like:**
- "Other projects competing for budget"
- "Not sure if this makes the cut"
- "Prioritizing other initiatives"

**Confidence:** Medium — competition for budget

**Interpretation:** Need to increase urgency or differentiate from competing projects.

### ROI Required

**What it looks like:**
- "Need to show ROI"
- "Business case required"
- "Has to pay for itself"
- "What's the payback?"

**Confidence:** Medium — hurdle identified

**Interpretation:** Build ROI model. Quantify their pain points.

---

## Comparison Signals

### Versus Current Costs

**What it looks like:**
- "Costs less than hiring"
- "Cheaper than our current solution"
- "If it saves us [current cost]..."

**Confidence:** Low to Medium — relative, not absolute

**Interpretation:** Understand their current costs to calibrate.

### Versus Alternatives

**What it looks like:**
- "[Competitor] quoted us $X"
- "Other solutions we've seen are around..."
- "Industry standard seems to be..."

**Confidence:** Medium — external reference point

**Interpretation:** Competitive intelligence. Price accordingly.

---

## Budget Signal Matrix

| Signal Type | Confidence | Next Step |
|-------------|------------|-----------|
| Exact amount, approved | High | Confirm fit, move to close |
| Range, approved | High | Calibrate to their range |
| Indicator only | Medium | Get more specific |
| Needs approval | Medium | Help build business case |
| Constrained | Medium | Prove ROI, creative pricing |
| Comparison only | Low | Understand their baseline |
| No signals | Low | More discovery needed |

---

## Budget Questions to Ask

When budget unclear, probe with:

1. "What budget range are you working with?"
2. "Has budget been allocated for this initiative?"
3. "Who needs to approve the investment?"
4. "What's your typical process for purchases like this?"
5. "What other solutions have you evaluated and at what price points?"

## Budget Red Flags

| Signal | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| Refuses to discuss budget | Not serious buyer or very early stage |
| Budget much lower than solution | Likely not a fit |
| "Budget is flexible" | May not be real budget holder |
| Budget keeps changing | Decision process unclear |
