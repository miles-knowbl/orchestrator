# Research Sources Reference

Where to find lead information.

## Company Information

### Website

**What to find:**
- Company description
- Products/services
- Target market
- Team page
- Careers page (hiring signals)
- News/blog

**Key sections:**
- About page
- Leadership team
- Careers (who are they hiring?)
- Contact (locations)

### LinkedIn Company Page

**What to find:**
- Employee count
- Growth rate
- Company updates
- Employee posts

**Signals:**
- Recent headcount changes
- Company posts (priorities)
- Hiring activity

### Crunchbase/PitchBook

**What to find:**
- Funding history
- Investors
- Employee count
- Founded date
- Leadership

**Signals:**
- Recent funding = growth pressure
- Investors = credibility context

### News

**What to find:**
- Recent press releases
- Industry news mentions
- Executive interviews
- Acquisitions

**Signals:**
- Growth announcements
- New initiatives
- Challenges mentioned

---

## Stakeholder Information

### LinkedIn

**What to find:**
- Name and title
- Career history
- Posts and activity
- Connections
- Content they engage with

**Signals:**
- Posts about challenges = pain points
- Shares about AI/automation = interest
- Recent role change = new priorities

### Twitter/X

**What to find:**
- Public opinions
- Topics of interest
- Industry engagement
- Conference attendance

### Conference/Speaking

**What to find:**
- Speaking topics
- Panel participation
- Conference attendance

**Signals:**
- Topics they speak on = priorities
- Conference choice = focus area

## Industry Research

### Industry Reports

**What to find:**
- Industry trends
- Common challenges
- Technology adoption
- Competitive landscape

### Industry News

**What to find:**
- Trends affecting the industry
- Regulatory changes
- Technology shifts
- Competitive dynamics

### Competitor Analysis

**What to find:**
- Who are their competitors?
- What solutions do competitors use?
- Industry benchmarks

---

## Technology Signals

### Job Postings

**What to find:**
- Tech stack (mentioned in job posts)
- Hiring for what roles?
- AI/ML mentions?

**Signals:**
- Hiring support roles = pain
- Hiring AI roles = already thinking about it
- Tech stack = integration context

### BuiltWith/SimilarTech

**What to find:**
- Technology stack
- Tools they use
- Integrations

**Signals:**
- Modern stack = easier implementation
- Specific CRM = integration opportunity

### G2/Capterra Reviews

**What to find:**
- Products they use
- Reviews they've left
- Pain points mentioned

## Pain Signals

### Hiring Patterns

| Signal | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| Hiring support roles | Support volume growing |
| Hiring CX leadership | CX is priority |
| AI/ML hiring | Already investing in AI |
| Many open roles | Growing fast |

### Company Activity

| Signal | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| Recent funding | Growth pressure |
| Expansion news | Scaling challenges |
| New product launch | Support demand increase |
| Acquisition | Integration challenges |

### Industry Context

| Signal | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| High-volume industry | Likely support pain |
| Regulated industry | Compliance needs |
| Seasonal industry | Spike challenges |
| High-churn industry | CX critical |

## Research Checklist

### Must Find

- [ ] Company name and website
- [ ] Industry and sub-industry
- [ ] Company size (employees)
- [ ] Key stakeholders (1-3)
- [ ] One personalization angle

### Should Find

- [ ] Funding/revenue if available
- [ ] Recent news
- [ ] Hiring patterns
- [ ] Tech stack clues
- [ ] Multiple pain hypotheses

### Nice to Have

- [ ] Mutual connections
- [ ] Executive interviews
- [ ] Industry reports
- [ ] Competitor context
- [ ] Conference attendance

## Research Efficiency

### Time Budget

| Priority | Research Time |
|----------|---------------|
| High priority lead | 20-30 minutes |
| Medium priority | 10-15 minutes |
| Low priority | 5 minutes (quick scan) |

### Quick Research (5 min)

1. Website homepage
2. LinkedIn company page
3. One key stakeholder
4. One pain hypothesis

### Standard Research (15 min)

1. Website (about, team, careers)
2. LinkedIn company + news
3. 2-3 stakeholders
4. Recent funding/news
5. 2-3 pain hypotheses

### Deep Research (30 min)

1. All standard items
2. Industry context
3. Tech stack signals
4. Multiple stakeholders
5. Personalization angles
6. Connection mapping
