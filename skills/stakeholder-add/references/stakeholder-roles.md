# Stakeholder Roles Reference

Detailed definitions for buying committee roles.

## Role: Champion

**Definition:** Your internal advocate who actively sells on your behalf when you're not in the room.

### Identification Signals

Strong Champion:
- Proactively shares information about internal dynamics
- Makes introductions without being asked
- Expresses urgency ("We need this")
- Asks "how can we make this happen?"
- Defends you against internal skeptics
- Provides competitive intelligence
- Coaches you on how to navigate their org

Moderate Champion:
- Responds quickly and thoroughly
- Shares some internal context
- Willing to make introductions when asked
- Engaged in meetings

Weak Champion:
- Interested but passive
- Doesn't advocate internally
- Waits for you to drive everything
- May be too junior to influence

### Your Job With Champions

1. **Enable them** — Give them ammunition (one-pagers, ROI calcs)
2. **Coach them** — Help them sell internally
3. **Protect them** — Don't make them look bad
4. **Appreciate them** — Acknowledge their help
5. **Keep them informed** — No surprises

### Champion Strength Scoring

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Executive Sponsor | C-level backing, can override objections |
| Strong | Senior with budget/decision authority, actively advocating |
| Moderate | Mid-level with some influence, engaged but limited power |
| Weak | Interested individual, no real authority |

## Role: Decision-Maker

**Definition:** Has authority to approve budget, sign contracts, or make final go/no-go decision.

### Identification Signals

- Title typically VP, Director, C-level, or "Head of"
- Others defer to their opinion
- Controls or influences budget discussions
- Asked for approval before proceeding
- Final sign-off mentioned

### Types of Decision-Makers

**Economic Buyer:**
- Controls the budget
- Cares about ROI, cost, business impact
- Usually CFO, VP, or budget owner

**Technical Buyer:**
- Approves technical fit
- Cares about integration, security, scalability
- Usually CTO, VP Engineering, IT Director

**Executive Sponsor:**
- Strategic decision authority
- Cares about vision, competitive advantage
- Usually C-level or SVP

### Your Job With Decision-Makers

1. **Build credibility** — They need to trust you
2. **Address their concerns** — Usually different from champions
3. **Speak their language** — Business impact for economic, technical depth for technical
4. **Respect their time** — Be concise, prepared
5. **Get access** — Many deals die without DM engagement

### Decision-Maker Engagement Risk

| Days Since Last DM Contact | Risk Level |
|---------------------------|------------|
| < 7 days | Low |
| 7-14 days | Medium |
| 14-30 days | High |
| > 30 days | Critical |

## Role: Influencer

**Definition:** Affects the decision through advice, evaluation, or usage but doesn't make final call.

### Types of Influencers

**Technical Evaluator:**
- Runs POCs, evaluates features
- Creates comparison scorecards
- Reports findings to decision-makers

**End User:**
- Will use the solution daily
- Can champion or block based on usability
- Often consulted by decision-makers

**Trusted Advisor:**
- Has decision-maker's ear
- May be consultant, long-time employee, or expert
- Opinion carries weight

**Peer:**
- Same level as decision-maker in different department
- May have implemented similar solutions
- Reference point for decision-maker

### Your Job With Influencers

1. **Win their support** — Make them advocates
2. **Address their criteria** — Understand what they're evaluating
3. **Make them look good** — Help them give positive reports
4. **Don't ignore them** — They can become blockers

---

## Role: Blocker

**Definition:** Actively or passively resisting the deal moving forward.

### Types of Blockers

**Active Blocker:**
- Openly opposes the solution
- Raises objections in meetings
- Advocates for alternatives

**Passive Blocker:**
- Doesn't engage
- Delays providing information
- Creates process obstacles
- "Busy" when meetings scheduled

**Competitive Blocker:**
- Favors a competitor
- May have existing relationship
- Introduces competitor late in process

**Status Quo Blocker:**
- Prefers to do nothing
- "If it ain't broke..."
- Risk-averse

### Your Job With Blockers

1. **Understand their concerns** — Often legitimate
2. **Address if possible** — Some concerns are solvable
3. **Isolate if necessary** — Work around if won't engage
4. **Convert if you can** — Blockers who flip become strong advocates
5. **Don't ignore** — They don't go away

### Blocker Handling Strategies

| Blocker Type | Strategy |
|--------------|----------|
| Technical concerns | Deep-dive, proof points, reference calls |
| Political/competitive | Find their win, executive alignment |
| Risk-averse | Risk mitigation, phased approach, guarantees |
| Budget-conscious | ROI proof, creative pricing, pilot |

## Role: Evaluator

**Definition:** Formally assessing the solution against defined requirements.

### Identification Signals

- Running POC or pilot
- Created RFP or evaluation criteria
- Comparing multiple vendors
- Building scorecards or matrices

### Your Job With Evaluators

1. **Understand criteria** — What are they scoring on?
2. **Provide proof** — Documentation, demos, references
3. **Be responsive** — Answer questions quickly and thoroughly
4. **Differentiate** — Make sure your strengths are weighted
5. **Build relationship** — They may become influencer or champion

## Buying Committee Patterns

### Healthy Deal
- Strong Champion
- At least one engaged Decision-Maker
- Evaluators reporting positive findings
- No active Blockers

### At-Risk Deal
- Weak Champion
- Decision-Makers not engaged
- Active Blocker with no mitigation
- Evaluators have unanswered concerns

### Red Flag Deal
- No Champion
- Can't access Decision-Makers
- Multiple Blockers
- Competitive Evaluator

## Role Evolution

Stakeholders can change roles:

| From | To | Why |
|------|-----|-----|
| Evaluator | Champion | Impressed by solution |
| Neutral DM | Supportive DM | Concerns addressed |
| Influencer | Blocker | Ignored or mishandled |
| Champion | Former Champion | Left company, changed role |

Update roles as you learn more—initial assessment often wrong.
