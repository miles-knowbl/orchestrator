# Weekly Structure Reference

How to structure a productive sales week.

## The Weekly Rhythm

### Monday: Plan & Prep

**Morning (2 hours):**
- Review pipeline snapshot
- Check deal priorities
- Identify week's top 3-5 priorities
- Schedule critical activities
- Prep for first meetings

**Afternoon:**
- Execute first priority actions
- Outreach for week's meetings
- Quick wins (follow-ups, check-ins)

### Tuesday-Wednesday: Execute

**Focus on:**
- Primary deals (Focus tier)
- Key meetings and calls
- Deep work activities
- Deal advancement

**Protect time for:**
- Discovery meetings
- Technical discussions
- Proposal work
- Champion enablement

### Thursday: Advance & Adjust

**Morning:**
- Continue execution
- Check progress on priorities
- Adjust if falling behind

**Afternoon:**
- Secondary deals (Maintain tier)
- Follow-up from earlier meetings
- Content creation if needed

### Friday: Close & Review

**Morning:**
- Final push on urgent items
- Close week's priorities
- Quick wins before weekend

**Afternoon (2 hours):**
- Weekly review
- Update deal files
- Prep next week's focus
- Clear inbox/admin

---

## Time Blocking

### Protected Time

Block these weekly:
- 2 hours: Monday planning
- 2 hours: Friday review
- 4-6 hours: Focus tier deep work
- 2-3 hours: Meeting prep
- 1-2 hours: Admin/email

### Meeting Windows

Designate specific times for meetings:
- External meetings: 10am-12pm, 2pm-4pm
- Internal meetings: Early morning or late afternoon
- No meetings: Deep work blocks

### Buffer Time

Always include:
- 30 min before important meetings
- 15 min between back-to-back
- 1 hour daily for unexpected

## Priority-Based Structure

### 3-5 Weekly Priorities

**Format:**
```
Priority #1: [Specific outcome]
- Deal: [Which deal]
- Actions: [What to do]
- Success: [How to know it worked]
```

**Example:**
```
Priority #1: Close BigTech contract
- Deal: BigTech ($350K)
- Actions: Legal follow-up, final version, signature
- Success: Signed contract by Friday
```

### Priority Types

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Close | Sign contract, close deal |
| Advance | Move to next stage |
| Recover | Fix at-risk deal |
| Qualify | Make go/no-go decision |
| Build | Create materials, enablement |

### Priority Distribution

Typical week:
- 1-2 Close priorities (near-term revenue)
- 1-2 Advance priorities (pipeline movement)
- 1 Recover/Qualify priority (pipeline health)

## Daily Structure

### Morning (8-12)

**Best for:**
- Deep work (proposals, prep)
- Important calls
- Creative work
- Focus tier deals

**Avoid:**
- Admin/email
- Low-priority tasks
- Reactive work

### Afternoon (1-5)

**Best for:**
- Meetings
- Follow-ups
- Maintain tier deals
- Collaborative work

**Later afternoon:**
- Quick tasks
- Email processing
- Next day prep

### End of Day (15 min)

- What got done?
- What didn't?
- What's critical tomorrow?
- Anything to prepare tonight?

---

## Energy Management

### High Energy Times

Use for:
- Most important work
- Difficult conversations
- Strategic thinking
- Complex proposals

### Lower Energy Times

Use for:
- Admin tasks
- Email
- Simple follow-ups
- Research

### Energy Traps

Avoid:
- Starting with email
- Meetings back-to-back all day
- No breaks between deep work
- Working on low-priority when tired

---

## Meeting Efficiency

### Before Meetings

- Clear objective (what must happen)
- Prep materials ready
- Questions planned
- Calendar blocked for prep

### During Meetings

- Start with objective
- Watch the clock
- Capture action items
- Confirm next steps

### After Meetings

- Debrief immediately (15 min)
- Send follow-up same day
- Update deal files
- Schedule next actions

---

## Weekly Capacity

### Realistic Expectations

| Activity | Hours/Week |
|----------|------------|
| External meetings | 8-12 |
| Meeting prep/debrief | 4-6 |
| Deep work (proposals, etc.) | 6-10 |
| Admin/email | 4-6 |
| Planning/review | 4 |
| Buffer/unexpected | 4-6 |
| **Total** | **30-44** |

### Maximum Meetings

- 3-4 external meetings/day max
- 6-8 meetings total/day max
- At least 2 meeting-free mornings/week

### Signs of Overload

- No deep work time
- Constant context switching
- Falling behind on follow-ups
- Missing prep time
- Weekend work required

---

## Adjusting Mid-Week

### When Behind

1. Re-prioritize (what's truly critical)
2. Delegate or defer
3. Shorten low-priority tasks
4. Cancel or reschedule non-essential
5. Communicate delays proactively

### When Ahead

1. Pull forward next week's work
2. Invest in high-value activities
3. Build enablement materials
4. Proactive outreach
5. Pipeline development

### Emergency Response

When urgent issue arises:
1. Assess real urgency
2. Reschedule non-critical
3. Focus on resolution
4. Catch up Friday or next week
5. Learn for future
