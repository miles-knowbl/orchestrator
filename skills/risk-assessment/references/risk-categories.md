# Risk Categories Reference

Types of risks that can derail deals.

## Champion Risk

### What Can Go Wrong

| Risk | Indicators | Impact |
|------|------------|--------|
| Champion leaves company | LinkedIn activity, vague about future | Critical — lose access |
| Champion changes roles | Mentions new responsibilities | High — lose authority |
| Champion loses influence | Political shifts mentioned | High — reduced advocacy |
| Champion goes cold | Response time increasing | High — lose momentum |
| Champion sentiment shifts | Tone changes, less enthusiasm | Medium — may become blocker |
| No backup champion | Single point of contact | High — fragile position |

### Early Warning Signs

- Longer response times
- Shorter, less enthusiastic messages
- Delegating to others
- Less proactive sharing
- "Let me check" increasing
- Avoids commitment

### Assessment Questions

1. How senior is the champion?
2. How engaged are they?
3. Do they have authority?
4. Is there a backup champion?
5. What's their tenure/stability?

## Competition Risk

### What Can Go Wrong

| Risk | Indicators | Impact |
|------|------------|--------|
| Competitor preferred | Positive competitor mentions | Critical — may lose |
| Active competitor evaluation | "Also talking to..." | High — head to head |
| Competitor undercutting | Price concerns emerge | Medium — margin pressure |
| Build vs. buy emerges | Internal option discussed | High — long delay or loss |
| Status quo wins | "Maybe later" | Medium — no decision |
| Late competitor entry | New vendor mentioned | Medium — extends cycle |

### Early Warning Signs

- Competitor name mentioned
- New technical requirements
- Request for RFP
- Unusual feature questions
- Price comparison requests
- Timeline extensions

### Assessment Questions

1. Who else are they evaluating?
2. What's their history with competitors?
3. Is build option on the table?
4. What would make them choose competitor?
5. What's our competitive advantage here?

## Budget Risk

### What Can Go Wrong

| Risk | Indicators | Impact |
|------|------------|--------|
| Budget not approved | "Need to get approval" | High — no deal |
| Budget cut | Reorg, layoffs mentioned | Critical — deal dies |
| Budget reallocated | Priority shifts | High — delayed or lost |
| Budget too small | Our price exceeds | Medium — scope cut |
| Budget timing mismatch | Fiscal year constraints | Medium — timing delay |
| ROI not proven | CFO skeptical | High — approval risk |

### Early Warning Signs

- Budget discussions avoided
- "Need to check with finance"
- Org changes announced
- Other priorities mentioned
- Price pushback intensifies
- CFO involvement blocked

### Assessment Questions

1. Is budget approved?
2. Who controls the budget?
3. What's the approval process?
4. What could cause budget cuts?
5. Is there budget competition?

## Timeline Risk

### What Can Go Wrong

| Risk | Indicators | Impact |
|------|------------|--------|
| Timeline slips | Missed milestones | Medium — extended cycle |
| Urgency drops | "No rush anymore" | High — deal may stall |
| Deadline missed | Hard deadline passes | Critical — window closes |
| Evaluation extends | More stakeholders | Medium — delayed decision |
| Dependencies delay | Other projects slip | Medium — start date moves |
| Decision-maker unavailable | Travel, leave | Medium — approval delayed |

### Early Warning Signs

- Meetings rescheduled
- "Let's revisit next quarter"
- Milestone dates move
- New stakeholders introduced
- Other projects prioritized
- Decision-maker absent

### Assessment Questions

1. Is the timeline real?
2. What's driving the deadline?
3. What happens if they miss it?
4. What could cause delays?
5. Are there dependencies?

## Technical Risk

### What Can Go Wrong

| Risk | Indicators | Impact |
|------|------------|--------|
| Integration fails | Complex systems, legacy | High — may be infeasible |
| Requirements change | Scope creep | Medium — reframe deal |
| Technical evaluation fails | Security, scalability | Critical — disqualified |
| Data not ready | Data quality issues | Medium — delayed start |
| IT blocks | Shadow IT pushback | High — internal resistance |
| Compliance issues | Regulatory concerns | High — may be disqualifying |

### Early Warning Signs

- New technical requirements
- Security questionnaire
- IT introduced late
- Data concerns raised
- Integration questions increase
- Compliance questions emerge

### Assessment Questions

1. What systems need to integrate?
2. What's the technical evaluation process?
3. Are there compliance requirements?
4. What's the data situation?
5. Who owns IT approval?

## Organizational Risk

### What Can Go Wrong

| Risk | Indicators | Impact |
|------|------------|--------|
| Reorg happens | News, rumors | High — everything resets |
| Key person leaves | LinkedIn, comments | High — lose relationships |
| Priority shifts | New initiatives | Medium — deprioritized |
| M&A activity | News, uncertainty | Critical — deal freezes |
| Budget freeze | Economic signals | Critical — deal stops |
| Political shifts | Internal conflicts | Medium — process slows |

### Early Warning Signs

- Org changes mentioned
- New leadership
- Strategy shifts
- Hiring freezes
- Unusual delays
- New stakeholders appear

### Assessment Questions

1. Is the organization stable?
2. Are there pending changes?
3. What's the company's financial health?
4. Are there competing initiatives?
5. What political dynamics exist?

## Fit Risk

### What Can Go Wrong

| Risk | Indicators | Impact |
|------|------------|--------|
| Wrong use case | Mismatch with capabilities | High — can't deliver |
| Expectations too high | Unrealistic targets | Medium — disappointed |
| Scope mismatch | Wants more than we offer | Medium — gap to close |
| Cultural mismatch | Working style conflicts | Low — friction |
| Wrong stakeholder | Solving wrong person's problem | High — won't close |
| Industry mismatch | Domain expertise gap | Medium — credibility |

### Early Warning Signs

- Features we don't have requested
- Expectations exceed capability
- Industry-specific needs
- Use case outside sweet spot
- Champion isn't problem owner

### Assessment Questions

1. Is this a good fit for our solution?
2. Can we deliver what they need?
3. Are expectations realistic?
4. Is this our target market?
5. Have we done this before?
