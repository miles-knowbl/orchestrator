# Risk Mitigation Reference

Strategies to address deal risks.

## Champion Risk Mitigation

### Single Champion Risk

**Strategy:** Build multiple relationships

**Actions:**
1. Request introductions from champion
2. Engage other stakeholders directly
3. Build relationship with champion's team
4. Connect with decision-makers
5. Identify and develop backup champion

**Prevention:**
- Always have 3+ relationships in an account
- Don't rely solely on champion for access
- Build executive relationships early

### Champion Going Cold

**Strategy:** Direct intervention

**Actions:**
1. Reach out directly (call, not email)
2. Ask explicitly: "I sense a shift, what's changed?"
3. Address underlying concerns
4. Re-engage with value
5. Consider executive intervention

**Prevention:**
- Regular check-ins
- Don't let gaps exceed 7 days
- Watch for early warning signs

### Champion Leaving

**Strategy:** Emergency succession planning

**Actions:**
1. Ask champion for handoff
2. Get introductions before they leave
3. Accelerate other relationships
4. Connect with champion's successor
5. Maintain relationship (may land elsewhere)

**Prevention:**
- Build multiple relationships
- Don't put all eggs in one basket
- Watch LinkedIn for signals

## Competition Risk Mitigation

### Active Competitive Evaluation

**Strategy:** Differentiate and accelerate

**Actions:**
1. Understand why they like competitor
2. Differentiate on your strengths
3. Provide relevant references
4. Accelerate your timeline
5. Arm champion with battle cards

**Prevention:**
- Know the competitive landscape
- Position proactively
- Build relationships before RFP

### Competitor Preferred

**Strategy:** Aggressive counter-positioning

**Actions:**
1. Understand their criteria for preference
2. Address those specific points
3. Offer aggressive pilot/pricing
4. Bring in executive support
5. Find internal advocates who prefer you

**Prevention:**
- Early differentiation
- Strong champion engagement
- Don't let competitor lead

### Build vs. Buy

**Strategy:** TCO and expertise positioning

**Actions:**
1. Present total cost of ownership analysis
2. Highlight time to value
3. Emphasize your expertise
4. Show opportunity cost of engineers building
5. Offer to augment their team (hybrid)

**Prevention:**
- Address early in discovery
- Understand their technical capabilities
- Position as extension of their team

## Budget Risk Mitigation

### Budget Not Approved

**Strategy:** Build the business case

**Actions:**
1. Create detailed ROI model
2. Quantify their current pain in dollars
3. Provide relevant case studies with numbers
4. Offer to present to finance
5. Propose phased approach to reduce risk

**Prevention:**
- Qualify budget early
- Understand approval process
- Engage economic buyer early

### Budget Cut

**Strategy:** Protect the deal

**Actions:**
1. Understand what's changed
2. Reduce scope to fit new reality
3. Propose phased implementation
4. Highlight cost of delay
5. Offer creative terms (pay over time, success-based)

**Prevention:**
- Monitor org health
- Have multiple deals in pipeline
- Build executive relationships

### ROI Concerns

**Strategy:** Prove value

**Actions:**
1. Create detailed ROI model specific to them
2. Offer paid pilot with defined success criteria
3. Provide references they can call
4. Propose success-based pricing elements
5. Document quick wins

**Prevention:**
- Lead with business outcomes
- Quantify pain early
- Know their metrics

## Timeline Risk Mitigation

### Timeline Slipping

**Strategy:** Create urgency

**Actions:**
1. Understand what's causing slip
2. Address blocking issues
3. Remind of original driver
4. Highlight cost of delay
5. Propose interim milestone

**Prevention:**
- Understand real driver for timeline
- Build urgency throughout
- Track milestones closely

### Urgency Dropped

**Strategy:** Re-establish stakes

**Actions:**
1. Ask what changed
2. Remind of original pain
3. Introduce new urgency (competitive, capability)
4. Offer incentive for faster decision
5. May need to step back if truly not urgent

**Prevention:**
- Understand true driver
- Document stakes early
- Regularly remind of impact

## Technical Risk Mitigation

### Integration Concerns

**Strategy:** Technical proof

**Actions:**
1. Bring in technical resources
2. Conduct integration assessment
3. Provide technical documentation
4. Offer integration POC
5. Reference similar integrations

**Prevention:**
- Technical discovery early
- Understand their stack
- Flag integration early

### Security/Compliance Concerns

**Strategy:** Address head-on

**Actions:**
1. Provide security documentation
2. Conduct security review
3. Offer compliance certifications
4. Reference similar customers
5. Engage their security team

**Prevention:**
- Know your compliance posture
- Have documentation ready
- Ask about requirements early

## Organizational Risk Mitigation

### Reorg in Progress

**Strategy:** Navigate carefully

**Actions:**
1. Understand new structure
2. Identify new decision-makers
3. Re-qualify opportunity
4. Build relationships with new players
5. Be patient but stay engaged

**Prevention:**
- Monitor news and signals
- Build multiple relationships
- Don't over-invest in single contacts

### Priority Shift

**Strategy:** Re-qualify

**Actions:**
1. Understand new priorities
2. Reposition against new priorities
3. Accept may need to pause
4. Maintain relationship
5. Be ready when priority returns

**Prevention:**
- Understand their priorities deeply
- Position as supporting key initiatives
- Build executive relationships

## General Mitigation Principles

### Proactive > Reactive

- Identify risks early
- Address before they escalate
- Monitor leading indicators
- Build relationships before you need them

### Multiple Strategies

- Don't rely on single mitigation
- Have backup plans
- Diversify relationships
- Cover multiple angles

### Accept Some Losses

- Not every risk can be mitigated
- Know when to walk away
- Cut losses early when appropriate
- Focus resources on winnable deals

### Document and Learn

- Track what works
- Learn from losses
- Update playbook
- Share with team
