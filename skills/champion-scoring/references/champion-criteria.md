# Champion Criteria Reference

What makes a strong champion.

## Champion Definition

A champion is your internal advocate who:
- **Believes** in your solution
- **Advocates** for you when you're not in the room
- **Navigates** internal politics on your behalf
- **Provides** information you wouldn't otherwise get
- **Accelerates** the buying process

## The MEDDIC Champion Test

A true champion meets these criteria:

1. **Access** — Has access to decision-makers
2. **Influence** — Can influence the decision
3. **Selling** — Actively sells for you internally
4. **Credibility** — Is respected within the organization
5. **Motivation** — Has personal stake in success

## Champion vs. Contact

| Champion | Contact |
|----------|---------|
| Advocates when you're gone | Answers questions when asked |
| Proactively helps | Responds reactively |
| Shares internal dynamics | Shares only what's asked |
| Makes introductions | Gives you names |
| Wants you to win | Is politely interested |

## Champion Strength Tiers

### Tier 1: Executive Sponsor

**Characteristics:**
- C-level or equivalent
- Ultimate decision authority
- Can override objections
- Organizational credibility
- Strategic vision alignment

**Example behaviors:**
- "I'll make this happen"
- Clears internal obstacles
- Signs off directly
- Assigns resources

**Score range:** 90-100

### Tier 2: Strong Champion

**Characteristics:**
- VP or Senior Director level
- Budget or significant authority
- Strong internal network
- Active advocacy
- Can influence C-suite

**Example behaviors:**
- Makes executive introductions
- Provides competitive intel
- Coaches on internal politics
- Follows up proactively
- Uses "we" when discussing solution

**Score range:** 70-89

### Tier 3: Moderate Champion

**Characteristics:**
- Director or Manager level
- Some influence in their area
- Engaged and helpful
- Limited organizational power
- May need air cover

**Example behaviors:**
- Advocates to their boss
- Shares information
- Responsive
- Wants to help but limited ability
- May need to "run it up"

**Score range:** 50-69

### Tier 4: Weak Champion

**Characteristics:**
- Manager or IC level
- Little organizational influence
- Interested but passive
- Can't move things forward
- More of a cheerleader

**Example behaviors:**
- "I love this but..."
- Waits for you to drive
- Can't make introductions happen
- Gets blocked by others
- Enthusiasm > ability

**Score range:** 30-49

### No Champion

**Characteristics:**
- Contact only
- Not advocating
- Passive participant
- Just doing their job
- No personal investment

**Example behaviors:**
- Answers questions only
- Doesn't follow up
- Doesn't share info
- Schedules meetings but doesn't drive
- "I'm just evaluating"

**Score range:** 0-29

## Champion Authority Matrix

| Authority Type | What They Can Do |
|----------------|------------------|
| Budget | Approve or influence spending |
| Technical | Approve or influence technical decisions |
| Process | Drive evaluation and procurement |
| Executive | Access and influence executives |
| Peer | Influence other stakeholders |

**Best champions** have multiple authority types.

## Champion Risk Factors

### Organizational Risk

| Risk | Impact |
|------|--------|
| Champion leaving company | Deal at serious risk |
| Champion changing roles | May lose authority/interest |
| Champion getting promoted | May hand off to someone else |
| Reorg affecting champion | Authority may shift |

### Relationship Risk

| Risk | Impact |
|------|--------|
| Champion goes cold | Need to diagnose why |
| Champion sentiment shifts | May become blocker |
| Champion loses credibility internally | Reduced influence |
| Champion prioritizes other things | Deal slows |

### Single Point of Failure

**Risk:** Only one champion, no backup

**Mitigation:**
- Build multiple relationships
- Identify backup champion
- Connect with champion's team
- Develop decision-maker relationships directly

## Finding a Champion

### Where to Look

1. **Problem owner** — Person most affected by the problem you solve
2. **Initiative owner** — Person driving the strategic initiative
3. **Budget owner** — Person who controls the money
4. **Previous success** — Person who championed similar solutions

### Questions to Identify

- "Who is most affected by this problem?"
- "Who owns the budget for this area?"
- "Who is driving the AI/digital transformation initiative?"
- "Who has successfully championed new technology before?"

### Qualification Questions

Ask potential champion:
- "How important is solving this to you personally?"
- "What happens if this doesn't get solved?"
- "Who needs to be convinced?"
- "What obstacles do you foresee?"
- "Are you willing to advocate for this internally?"

What champions need from you:

|------|-----------------|
| Ammunition | One-pagers, ROI calculators, case studies |
| Talking points | Key messages for each stakeholder |
| Objection handling | Responses to likely pushback |
| Credibility | References, proof points, demos |
| Air cover | Executive engagement, guarantees |
