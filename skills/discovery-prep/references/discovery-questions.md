# Discovery Questions Reference

Question frameworks for effective discovery.

## Problem Discovery Questions

### Situation Questions

Understand their current state:

1. "Can you describe how you handle [problem area] today?"
2. "Walk me through the process from start to finish"
3. "How many [tickets/interactions] do you handle per [day/month]?"
4. "Who is involved in this process?"
5. "What tools do you use currently?"

### Problem Questions

Uncover pain and challenges:

1. "What's the biggest challenge with the current approach?"
2. "What would you change if you could?"
3. "Where do things break down?"
4. "What frustrates your team most?"
5. "What takes longer than it should?"

### Impact Questions

Quantify the problem:

1. "How does this impact [revenue/customers/team]?"
2. "What does this cost you in [time/money/resources]?"
3. "What happens when things go wrong?"
4. "How does this affect customer satisfaction?"
5. "What opportunities are you missing because of this?"

### Urgency Questions

Understand timeline drivers:

1. "Why is this a priority now?"
2. "What happens if you don't solve this in [timeframe]?"
3. "What's changed that makes this important?"
4. "Is there a specific deadline driving this?"
5. "What's the cost of waiting?"

## Solution Discovery Questions

### Vision Questions

Understand desired state:

1. "What would success look like for you?"
2. "In an ideal world, how would this work?"
3. "If this worked perfectly, what would be different?"
4. "What metrics would you expect to improve?"
5. "How would your team's work change?"

### Requirements Questions

Define scope:

1. "What specific [interactions/tasks] would you want automated?"
2. "What's in scope vs. out of scope?"
3. "What must the solution do vs. nice to have?"
4. "Are there any non-negotiable requirements?"
5. "What integrations are needed?"

### Constraint Questions

Identify limitations:

1. "What technical constraints should we know about?"
2. "Are there security or compliance requirements?"
3. "What's your budget range for this?"
4. "When do you need this live?"
5. "What resources can you dedicate to implementation?"

## Stakeholder Discovery Questions

### Decision Process

Understand how they buy:

1. "Besides yourself, who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
2. "What's your typical process for evaluating solutions like this?"
3. "Who has final sign-off authority?"
4. "Are there other teams that would be affected?"
5. "What would cause this to stall?"

### Influencer Mapping

Identify the players:

1. "Who would be the day-to-day users of this?"
2. "Who controls the budget for this initiative?"
3. "Who would need to approve the technical implementation?"
4. "Is there anyone who might have concerns?"
5. "Who has championed successful projects like this before?"

### Internal Context

Understand politics:

1. "How does this fit with other company priorities?"
2. "Are there competing initiatives for resources?"
3. "What's the executive team's view on AI/automation?"
4. "Have you tried something like this before?"
5. "What would make someone internally skeptical?"

## Technical Discovery Questions

### Current State

Understand their environment:

1. "What's your current tech stack for [area]?"
2. "What CRM/support platform do you use?"
3. "How do your systems integrate today?"
4. "Do you have APIs available for integration?"
5. "What does your data infrastructure look like?"

### Requirements

Define technical needs:

1. "What authentication does your team use (SSO, etc.)?"
2. "Are there data residency requirements?"
3. "What security certifications do you require?"
4. "What uptime/SLA do you need?"
5. "How do you handle [specific technical concern]?"

### Readiness

Assess implementation ability:

1. "Do you have technical resources to support implementation?"
2. "What's your change management process?"
3. "How have past technology implementations gone?"
4. "Who would own this technically after launch?"
5. "What does your testing/QA process look like?"

## Competitive Discovery Questions

### Landscape

Understand alternatives:

1. "What other solutions are you considering?"
2. "Have you evaluated [competitor] or similar tools?"
3. "Is building this in-house an option you're considering?"
4. "What's your current solution and why are you looking to change?"
5. "What do you like/dislike about alternatives you've seen?"

### Criteria

Understand evaluation:

1. "What's most important in your decision criteria?"
2. "What would make you choose us over alternatives?"
3. "What concerns do you have about solutions you've seen?"
4. "How are you comparing different options?"
5. "What would be a dealbreaker?"

## Question Sequencing

### Opening (0-10 min)

- Build rapport
- Confirm agenda
- Broad situation questions

### Middle (10-35 min)

- Problem questions (dig into pain)
- Impact questions (quantify)
- Technical questions
- Stakeholder questions

### Closing (35-45 min)

- Solution vision questions
- Next step questions
- Timeline confirmation

---

## Question Types to Avoid

| Type | Example | Why |
|------|---------|-----|
| Yes/No questions | "Is this a problem?" | Doesn't generate insight |
| Leading questions | "You probably struggle with X, right?" | Puts words in mouth |
| Multiple questions | "What about X and Y and Z?" | Confuses |
| Jargon-heavy | "How's your NPS impacted by AHT?" | May not understand |
| Assumptive | "When you implement this..." | Assumes the sale |
