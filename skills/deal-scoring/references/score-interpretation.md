# Score Interpretation Reference

What scores mean and how to act on them.

## Deal Health Interpretation

### 80-100: Excellent

**What it means:**
- Deal is highly qualified
- Strong champion and decision-maker engagement
- Clear use case and timeline
- Budget confirmed
- Low risk of loss

**Action:**
- Fast-track to close
- Remove any friction from buying process
- Don't over-sell — they're ready
- Focus on logistics, not convincing
- Protect the deal (don't mess it up)

### 60-79: Good

**What it means:**
- Solid deal with some gaps
- Most fundamentals in place
- 1-2 areas need attention
- Worth investing time

**Action:**
- Identify the gap(s)
- Focus efforts on improving weak scores
- Keep momentum
- Address risks before they derail
- Normal sales process with focus

### 40-59: Moderate

**What it means:**
- Deal has potential but significant gaps
- May be missing champion, budget, or timeline
- Higher risk
- Requires more work to close

**Action:**
- Diagnose: What's holding it back?
- Decide: Is it worth the effort?
- If yes: Focus on biggest gaps
- If no: Deprioritize, keep warm
- May need to create urgency or find champion

### 20-39: Weak

**What it means:**
- Deal has fundamental problems
- Missing multiple key elements
- High risk of loss or stall
- Questionable qualification

**Action:**
- Re-qualify: Is this really a deal?
- Identify: What would make this viable?
- Honest conversation with prospect
- Consider deprioritizing
- May be better to walk away

### 0-19: Poor

**What it means:**
- Not qualified
- Missing most key elements
- Very unlikely to close
- Time sink

**Action:**
- Disqualify
- Move to nurture track
- Don't invest selling time
- Check back in 6+ months
- Focus resources elsewhere

## Component Score Interpretation

### AI Readiness Interpretation

| Score | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 80-100 | High | Can self-implement with guidance |
| 60-79 | Medium | Will need support, standard path |
| 40-59 | Low | Needs significant enablement |
| 0-39 | Not Ready | May fail without heavy support |

**Low AI Readiness Actions:**
- Offer more managed/turnkey approach
- Set realistic implementation expectations
- Budget for more support
- Consider education phase before sale

### Deal Confidence Interpretation

| Score | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 80-100 | High | Very likely to close |
| 60-79 | Medium | Good chance, some uncertainty |
| 40-59 | Low | Uncertain, needs work |
| 0-39 | Very Low | Unlikely without major change |

**Low Deal Confidence Actions:**
- Identify the missing element (budget? timeline? champion?)
- Address that specific gap
- May need executive engagement
- Consider qualification status

### Champion Strength Interpretation

| Score | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 80-100 | Strong | Reliable internal advocate |
| 60-79 | Moderate | Helpful but limited influence |
| 40-59 | Weak | Contact but not true champion |
| 0-39 | None | No internal advocacy |

**Weak Champion Actions:**
- Find a stronger champion
- Enable current contact with materials
- Build relationship with decision-makers directly
- Consider if deal is viable without champion

### Use Case Clarity Interpretation

| Score | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 80-100 | Scoped | Ready for proposal/pilot |
| 60-79 | Defined | Clear direction, details TBD |
| 40-59 | Exploring | General interest, needs shaping |
| 0-39 | Vague | "We need AI" — no specifics |

**Low Clarity Actions:**
- Discovery workshop
- Help them define use case
- Share case studies for inspiration
- Narrow scope before proceeding

## Score Trend Interpretation

### Improving

**Signals:**
- Scores increasing over time
- Gaps being addressed
- Engagement deepening
- Deal progressing

**Action:**
- Keep momentum
- Don't slow down
- Address remaining gaps
- Move toward close

### Stable

**Signals:**
- Scores flat over time
- Deal not progressing
- Same objections recurring
- Stuck

**Action:**
- Diagnose stall
- Try new approach
- Executive intervention?
- Assess if fixable

### Declining

**Signals:**
- Scores decreasing
- Engagement dropping
- New concerns emerging
- Champion going cold

**Action:**
- Urgent intervention
- Direct conversation about what changed
- May need to reset
- Consider walking away if unfixable

## Score Combination Patterns

### High Confidence, Low Readiness

"Will buy but may fail to implement"

**Risk:** Bad customer, implementation problems
**Action:** Set realistic expectations, plan for extra support

### Low Confidence, High Readiness

"Great fit but not progressing"

**Risk:** Losing to competitor or status quo
**Action:** Create urgency, engage executive, differentiate

### Strong Champion, Low DM Engagement

"Champion selling but can't get decision"

**Risk:** Deal stalls at approval stage
**Action:** Help champion get executive access, provide materials

### High Clarity, Low Budget

"Know what they want but can't afford"

**Risk:** Scope reduction or no deal
**Action:** Creative pricing, phased approach, ROI focus

## Score-Based Pipeline Segmentation

| Segment | Deal Health | Strategy |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Fast Track | 80+ | Minimize friction, close quickly |
| Work | 60-79 | Focus efforts, address gaps |
| Nurture | 40-59 | Lower effort, watch for change |
| Disqualify | <40 | Don't invest, revisit later |

---

## Red Flags

Regardless of overall score, watch for:

| Red Flag | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Score dropped >20 points | Urgent investigation |
| Champion score drops | Talk to champion directly |
| Timeline extends | Re-qualify urgency |
| Budget disappears | Re-qualify opportunity |
| New blocker appears | Address immediately |
